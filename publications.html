A Brief Research Summary

A formal article on my research summary will be downloaded soon.

Since September 2012, I co-founded the first Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (AI-Lab) at Beijing Forestry University, and became a research assistant. Fortunately, I worked with Yanyan Xu (Beijing Forestry University) and Dengfeng Ke (Chinese Academy of Sciences) and became the team leader of project called Automated Chinese Essay Scoring (ACES). The first part is to correct erroneous Chinese characters, using N-gram Langauge Model and Weighted Finite-State Transducer (WFST). This project can be used independently in Chinese text proofread, or in scoring essays from characters level. The second part is to develop some novel techniques to understand the essays and to score them based on Pattern Recognition, Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning. Not only did we improve the computational efficiency of conventional methods (Incremental LSA, ILSA) and apply some strategies from other fields like topic modeling (Regularized Latent Semantic Indexing, RLSI), but proposed our new method (Contextualized Latent Semantic Indexing, CLSI). By these projects, we make our own contribution to the MHK tests, the Minority-oriented Chinese Test as Second Language.
