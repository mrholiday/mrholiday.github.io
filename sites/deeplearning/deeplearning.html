Fundamental Readings

Our weekly seminars will be based on these readings, so as a prerequisite, read through the publications shown here. After read, you should know what is a neural netoworks for NLP going to do; what are "window" and "sentence" approaches; and what is a convolutional layer or network. My recommendation is that the tutorial on NAACL 2013 is a very good start -- it's easy to read, funny and clear. Then following the idea presented, you can read publications by Ronan Collobert et al., Yoshua Bengio et al., and new work in ACL 2014 by Nal Kalchbrenner et al. If you have any good suggestions (like you read a very good paper), you can contact me by email. Again, please read the publications in the order we list here.

  • Deep Learning for NLP (without magic), Richard Socher, and Christopher Manning, NAACL 2013. Atalanda, GA, USA;
  • A Neural Probabilistic Language Model, Yoshua Bengio, Rejean Duchrame, Pascal Vincent, and Christian Jauvin, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 2003, 3: 1137--1155;
  • A Unified Architecture for Natural Language Processing: Deep Neural Networks with Multitask Learning, Ronan Collobert, and Jason Weston, ICML 2008. Helsinki, Finland;
  • Natural Language Processing (Almost) from Scratch (accepted version), Ronan Collobert, Jason Weston, Leon Bottou, Michael Karlen, Koray Kavukcuoglu, and Pavel Kuksa, Journal of Machine Learning Research, 2011, 12: 2493--2537;
  • Natural Language Processing (Almost) from Scratch (submitted version), Ronan Collobert, Jason Weston, Leon Bottou, Michael Karlen, Koray Kavukcuoglu, and Pavel Kuksa, Journal of Machine Learning Research;
  • A Convolutional Neural Network for Modelling Sentences, Nal Kalchbrenner, Edward Grefenstette, and Phil Blunsom, ACL 2014. Baltimore, MD, USA;
  • GloVe: Global Vectors for Word Representation, Jeffrey Pennington, Richard Socher, and Christopher D. Manning, EMNLP 2014. Doha, Qatar;
  • Semi-Supervised Recursive Autoencoders for Predicting Sentiment Distributions, Richard Socher, Jeffrey Pennington, Eric H. Huang, Andrew Y. Ng, and Christopher D. Manning, EMNLP 2010. MIT, Massachusetts, USA;
  • Max-Margin Tensor Neural Network for Chinese Word Segmentation, Wenzhe Pei, Tao Ge, and Baobao Chang, ACL 2014. Baltimore, MD, USA;
